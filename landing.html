FAQ

Common questions

TrustPay holds the buyer's payment from the moment it's confirmed until the buyer approves the delivery or a dispute is resolved. Neither party can access it directly in between.

The buyer can raise a dispute during the inspection period and upload photos or other evidence. The seller can respond, and the TrustPay team decides whether to release the funds or refund the buyer.

A flat 2% of the item price, charged only when a deal actually goes through. There's no cost for browsing, messaging, or cancelling before payment.

Identity verification (KYC) is optional at first, mainly to build your reputation score faster. It may become required for higher-value escrows later on.

Payments currently go through manual confirmation while we finish integrating bKash, Nagad, bank transfer, and card payments.
